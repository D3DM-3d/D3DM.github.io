D3DM: High-Fidelity Image-to-3D Generation with Direct 3D Diffusion Model

Anonymous
arXiv Code

D3DM generates image-to-3D samples with impressive precision. Given a single-view image, it can produce fine-grained geometric details and high-quality 3D shapes within a matter of seconds.


Abstract

Diffusion Models have shown significant promise in generating high-fidelity 3D shapes from a single image. However, previous methods primarily rely on image-to-multiview-to-3D approaches, enhancing 3D consistency through multi-view diffusion models. These methods are constrained by the quality of the generated multiview images and are susceptible to cumulative errors. Besides, these methods often face inconsistencies and pose uncertainties between generated 3D shapes and input images, which diminish the model's overall controllability. To address these challenges, we propose a Direct 3D Diffusion Model (D3DM). D3DM utilizes a Cross-modal Variational Autoencoder (CVAE) to encode each shape into latent space, featuring a Shape-Image Cross-modal Attention (SICA) that dynamically balances the influence of 3D features with 2D image information. In addition, Normal Space Sampling (NSS) enhances the performance of the CVAE to capture fine details, particularly edges and sharp features. To guarantee the pose controllability of the generated 3D shapes, we introduce a Rotation-Invariant technique that ensures directional consistency between the mesh and the image. Furthermore, D3DM utilizes a Pixel-Semantic co-guidance mechanism to generate high-quality meshes by integrating the global semantics from CLIP with the pixel-level details from DINOv2. Extensive experiments demonstrate that D3DM is capable of generating high-fidelity 3D shapes from single-view image.


Method Overview

D3DM begins by training a Cross-modal Variational Autoencoder (CVAE) to encode each shape into a latent space, producing a shape latent embedding z. This embedding is then decoded to accurately predict the occupancy values for query points, enabling the generation of high-quality 3D shapes using the Marching Cubes algorithm. Building on the pre-trained CVAE, we develop a 3D latent diffusion model to learn the shape latent embedding from an input image. During inference, D3DM can generate high-quality 3D shapes from just a single image in a straightforward feed-forward process.
